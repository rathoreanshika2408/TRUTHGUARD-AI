Tactic 01
🚨 Urgency triggers
Fake messages create artificial time pressure to stop you from thinking critically. Phrases like "Share in 2 hours", "अभी करें वरना मौका जाएगा", or "BREAKING — expires tonight" are classic red flags. Real important news doesn't disappear in 2 hours. If a message pressures you to act immediately — pause and verify first.
Tactic 02
😱 Fear-based messaging
Content designed to scare you overrides rational thinking. Fake health cures, disease outbreak hoaxes, crime exaggeration, and religious threat messages all weaponize fear. The formula: create panic → bypass critical thinking → get shares. Always ask: does a verified news source confirm this?
Tactic 03
🏛️ Fake government schemes
India's most dangerous scam category. Messages claim the government is giving free money, phones, gas cylinders, or laptops. Real government schemes are announced only on pib.gov.in — never through WhatsApp forwards. Never click unofficial links or share your Aadhaar, OTP, or bank details.

5 golden rules
1
Always check pib.gov.in for government scheme claims
2
Never share your Aadhaar, OTP, or bank details from a forward
3
If it says "forward to 10 people" — it's almost certainly fake
4
Check boomlive.in or altnews.in before believing viral claims
5
When in doubt — don't share. Sharing fake news makes you part of the problem
